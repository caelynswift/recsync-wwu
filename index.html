Each cell shows a score from 0 to 100 representing how busy the Rec Center typically is at that hour. How these recommendations are calculated

Each time slot is scored across three dimensions: time match (how well the slot aligns with your stated preference), crowd fit (adjusted by your tolerance — quiet preference means low-congestion slots score higher, crowd-lover preference inverts this), and equipment availability (based on observed utilization rates at that hour). The three unique days with the highest combined scores are returned.

Honest limitations: This model is trained on synthetic data and doesn't reflect real-time scans, WWU finals week, quarter transitions, or class-schedule-driven patterns. It performs best for users with consistent preferences and standard schedules. One-off situations and edge cases won't be well-represented.

About RecSync

MIS 432 — AI in Business  •  Western Washington University  •  2025

Project Overview

RecSync is a data-driven gym companion built for Western Washington University students. It combines crowd forecasting with preference-based recommendations to help Vikings — especially those new to campus — make the most of the Bellingham Recreation Center.

The system addresses a real gap: new students are often hesitant to visit the Rec Center because they don't know what to expect. RecSync reduces that friction with transparent, data-backed predictions and optional partner matching.

System Type

Recommendation system: Matches student preferences (time of day, equipment type, crowd tolerance) against per-hour congestion forecasts to surface optimal gym windows.

Forecasting model: Aggregates 300 synthetic visit records into average member counts per hour block, normalized into a 0–100 congestion score for each day-hour combination.

Demo Scenario Results

  • Morning free weights, avoids crowds (Scenario A): System recommends Tuesday and Friday 8–9am — both show congestion under 25. Squat racks and dumbbell areas are historically underutilized at those hours. Strong time match, maximum crowd score for low tolerance.
  • Evening machines, fine with crowds (Scenario B): Recommends Wednesday and Monday 5–6pm — peak congestion (67–88), which scores well for high-tolerance users. Cable machines and leg press show moderate utilization.
  • Saturday beginner cardio (Scenario C): Recommends Saturday 8am — treadmills and ellipticals free before the weekend mid-morning surge. Saturday peaks at 11am–2pm; the 8am window is consistently the quietest entry point.

Where it works well: Consistent schedules, equipment-specific preferences, crowd-sensitive users, students planning their first visit.

Where it falls short: Real-time availability, special events, finals week patterns, highly variable schedules, or users whose goals change week-to-week. The synthetic data doesn't capture seasonal WWU patterns.

Privacy & Data

  • All visit data used in RecSync is synthetic — generated to reflect realistic WWU Rec Center traffic patterns, not sourced from real student records.
  • No real student data, ID scan records, or personally identifiable information is used or stored.
  • Profile information entered into RecSync exists only in your browser session and is never transmitted to a server.
  • Workout goals are used internally for recommendation scoring only — they are never displayed on partner-facing cards.
  • A production deployment would require IRB approval, FERPA compliance review, and formal data sharing agreements with the WWU Recreation Center.
